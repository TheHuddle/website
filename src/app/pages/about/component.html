About "The Huddle"

At first, open source can seem scary; contribution guidelines, licenses, and git history strategies are intimidating hurdles to clear. Our goal is to help you learn and practice open source best-practices in fun bite-sized chunks!

Whether you're an experienced core-contributor or just starting your journey into open-source, our community has a place for you.

How to Participate

In addition to our goal to create a welcoming community, we love to hold events to promote open source and education! Please remember that all server interaction and event submissions are expected to follow our code of conduct!

Events

Events will be announced in the #events channel on discord. Each announcement will detail

  • how to make a submission,
  • what prize(s) can be won,
  • any event-specific rules,
  • and a deadline.

Any submissions received after the deadline are not eligible for prizes.*

Discord and Website Access

You must register and verify an account with us in order to view the discord and log in to the community website. Please fill out our registration form, and, provided you are human, you will receive an acceptance email and log-in credentials within 48 hours. If you have not received an email in this time and are still unable to access the discord, please reach out through our community email.
